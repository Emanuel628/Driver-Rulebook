# Legal Disclaimer

## Required Positioning

Driver Rulebook is an educational reference app.

It must never present itself as:

- legal advice
- official DOT training
- official FMCSA training
- official PHMSA training
- official OSHA training
- an FMCSA-certified ELD
- a replacement for required records of duty status
- a replacement for shipping papers
- a replacement for SDS documents
- a final hazmat compliance decision tool
- a replacement for company safety procedures

## Core Disclaimer

Recommended app-wide disclaimer:

> Driver Rulebook is an educational reference only. It is not legal advice, not official DOT/FMCSA/PHMSA/OSHA training, not an FMCSA-certified ELD, and not a substitute for shipping papers, SDS documents, company procedures, current regulations, or qualified safety personnel.

## HOS Disclaimer

Recommended HOS disclaimer:

> HOS explanations are provided for education only. This app does not track legal driving logs, does not replace an ELD, and does not determine official compliance status.

## Hazmat / Placard Disclaimer

Recommended hazmat disclaimer:

> Hazmat and placard explanations are provided for education only. Placard and identification-number decisions must be confirmed using current regulations, shipping papers, package markings, carrier procedures, and qualified hazmat personnel.

## GHS / SDS Disclaimer

Recommended GHS/SDS disclaimer:

> GHS and SDS explanations are provided for education only. GHS workplace labels and SDS information do not automatically determine DOT transportation classification or placarding requirements.

## Result Language

Guided explanations should use cautious language:

Use:

- This rule may apply.
- Check the shipping paper first.
- Confirm with company safety or dispatch.
- A plain placard may not be enough.
- Based on the information entered, review this requirement.

Avoid:

- You are compliant.
- You are legal.
- This is guaranteed correct.
- You do not need placards.
- This replaces your ELD.

## App Store Description Warning

Do not imply government affiliation.

Avoid official-sounding app names or descriptions that could suggest the app is made by DOT, FMCSA, PHMSA, or OSHA.
